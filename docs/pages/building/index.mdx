# Building [Guidance on the building Binius constraint systems]

Binius is a proof system framework that can be found and downloaded [here](https://gitlab.com/IrreducibleOSS/binius). To build on Binius you must build a constraint system. The following pages of documentation are intended as guidance for building Binius constraint systems, and connecting them with Binius [`prove`](https://docs.binius.xyz/binius_core/constraint_system/fn.prove.html) and [`verify`](https://docs.binius.xyz/binius_core/constraint_system/fn.verify.html) functions for a complete proof system.

## Introductory Content

In our page about [constraint systems](/building/constraints), we outline the creation of a constraint system, and its interaction with Binius's `prove` and `verify` functions. We moreover present a simple example exhibiting a [bitwise constraint](/building/bitwise).

## Site Directory

Below, we sketch this site area's sections in slightly more detail.

<Callout type="note">
<Collapsible>
[**The Basic Pattern.**](pattern) Here, we explain our fundamental constraint-building recipe: the _gadget pattern_.

Indeed, anyone buliding constraints on Binius must ultimately use this gadget pattern repeatedly. Our gadget pattern involves three main steps: [declaring](pattern/declaring) columns, [populating](pattern/populating) them with values, and finally [constraining](pattern/constraining) them. We explain each of these steps in detail, clarifying which steps must be performed by _which_ among the two parties, and how to achieve them in code.
</Collapsible>
</Callout>

<Callout type="note">
<Collapsible>
[**A Lookup Construction.**](lookup) Here, we undertake an extended, worked example, based on a simple _lookup_ protocol.

Indeed, lookups are key and important. In this site area, we build a lookup from scratch, using a simple technique. The technique we pursue here is _simpler_ than the [Lasso-based approach](/basics/arithmetization/examples/lasso) we pursue in this site's _basics_ area. It's less efficient for the verifier, but is powerful enough to showcase some key ideas.
</Collapsible>
</Callout>

## Further Content

We close off this site area by dropping a few further miscellaneous pages. In _[making transparents](transparents)_, we discuss _explicit transparent_ columns, columns made up exclusively of ad-hoc, verifier-supplied concrete values. These constructions are important in more advanced lookup protocols, like [Lasso](/basics/arithmetization/examples/lasso).

Finally, in our [Collatz page](/building/collatz), we take a practice-oriented tour back through our [Collatz arithmetization](/basics/arithmetization/matching/collatz), already developed in our _basics_ site area. This example gives us a concrete look at channels and flushing rules, key concepts in our [M3 arithmetization schema](/basics/arithmetization/matching).

Beyond these simple guiding examples, many more examples of Binius constraint systems can be found in our [Binius circuits](https://gitlab.com/IrreducibleOSS/binius/-/tree/main/crates/circuits/src) directory. For examples involving full prove and verify functionality, we recommend our [Binius examples](https://gitlab.com/IrreducibleOSS/binius/-/tree/main/examples).
