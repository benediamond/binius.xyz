# The Rest [The univariate skip's positive rounds]

As of the end of the [univariate skip](/reductions/zerocheck/univariate), the parties have obtained an _updated_ sum claim $S \coloneqq g(s')$. Moreover, the prover has locally specialized his rectangular multilinears $\widehat{t}_0, \ldots , \widehat{t}_{n - 1}$. That is, for each $\widehat{t}_i$, using the verifier's univariate challenge $s'$, the prover has specialised $\widehat{t}_i(Y, X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - k - 1})$ to $\widehat{t}_i(s', X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - k - 1})$. This latter polynomial—in fact, it's a multilinear!—requires just $2^{\ell - k}$ values to represent, as opposed to the $2^\ell$ we started with.

Moreover, the claim we're interested in checking is simply that:

$$S \stackrel{?}= \sum_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell} \widehat{C}(s', v_0, \ldots , v_{\ell - k - 1}) \cdot \delta_D(s', s') \cdot \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(v, r)$$.

This is mechanically identical to a _normal, rectangular_ sumcheck claim, pertaining now to the $\ell - k$-variate multilinears $\widehat{t}_i(s', X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - k - 1})$ and the usual composition polynomial $c(Z_0, \ldots , Z_{n - 1})$.
