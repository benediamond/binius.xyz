# Multiset-Check [Reducing multiset equality to grand-product claims]

As [we've mentioned](/arithmetization/matching/verifying) already, the _equality of multisets_ turns out to be our way of mathematically capturing the balancing of M3 [channels](/arithmetization/matching/definition#channels). Here, we sketch how we process multiset-equality claims in Binius (specifically, how we reduce them in turn to [grand-product](/reductions/gpa) claims).

The first thing that makes this tricky is that channels in general accept _tuple types_. Thus we fix a channel and write $\mu$ for its tuple arity (i.e., the number of _components_ in each tuple pushed to or pulled from that channel). The channel's two internal multisets, then, will look something like $(T_0, \ldots , T_{\mu - 1})$ and $(U_0, \ldots , U_{\mu - 1})$, respectively ($T$s for its pushes and $U$s for its pulls, let's say). Each of the individual columns $T_i$ and $U_i$ will be an $\ell$-variate multilinear, let's say, and will _itself_ be virtual.

Indeed, remember that each channel, in general, will receive pushes and pulls at the hands of _multiple different_ flushing rules. When two or more _different_ flushing rules push or pull (as the case may be) to a single channel, the resulting _multiset_ of pushed or pulled (respectively) tuples will itself be virtual, constructed as a [merge](/reductions/virtual/merge). That is, each _individual_ $T_i$ or $U_i$ will itself be a merge, with its halves (etc.) coming from disparate flushing rules.

In any case, we ignore for now how these $T_i$s and $U_i$s are constructed, and just note for now that they're virtual. The equality we're interested in checking is:

$$\left\{ (T_0(v), \ldots , T_{\mu - 1}(v)) \mid v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell \right\} \stackrel{?}= \left\{ (U_0(v), \ldots , U_{\mu - 1}(v)) \mid v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell \right\}$$,

where both sides of the above equality are understood as _multisets_ (i.e., with _multiplicity_ counted).

## The Fingerprinting Step

The first step will be to "de-tuplify" the two multisets at hand. Here, we are going to reduce the problem of matching multisets whose _elements_ are tuples (i.e., with $\mu$ components each) to the problem of matching multisets whose elements are just singleton field elements.

Indeed, it is shown in [[DP23](https://eprint.iacr.org/2023/1784.pdf), Prot. 4.20] that, to check whether the above multiset equality holds, it suffices for the verifier—up to a soundness error on the order of $\frac{\mu \cdot 2^\ell}{|\mathcal{T}_\tau|}$—to sample a random fingerprinting challenge $s \gets \mathcal{T}_\tau$, and to check instead whether the two virtual polynomials $\sum_{i = 0}^{\mu - 1} s^i \cdot T_i$ and $\sum_{i = 0}^{\mu - 1} s^i \cdot U_i$ yield equal multisets over $\mathcal{B}_\ell$. Note that we have replaced the _tuples_ $(T_0, \ldots , T_{\mu - 1})$ and $(U_0, \ldots , U_{\mu - 1})$ of virtual columns with the _single_ virtual columns $\sum_{i = 0}^{\mu - 1} s^i \cdot T_i$ and $\sum_{i = 0}^{\mu - 1} s^i \cdot U_i$. These things are _linear combination_ virtual polynomials, something we're [allowed to do](/reductions/virtual).

Unrolling this, we see that our claim is now the equality of $1$-ary multisets:

$$\left\{ \sum_{i = 0}^{\mu - 1} s^i \cdot T_i(v) \mid v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell \right\} \stackrel{?}= \left\{ \sum_{i = 0}^{\mu - 1} s^i \cdot U_i(v) \mid v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell \right\}$$.

The point here is that we've made progress, since these are multisets of singletons, instead of multisets of tuples.

## The Permutation Step

Zooming into a single channel, we write $(T_0, \ldots , T_{\mu - 1})$ and $(U_0, \ldots , U_{\mu - 1})$ for the _pair_ of tuples of $\ell$-variate virtual polynomials at issue. The _multiset equality_ claim entails that the multisets of values taken by these tuples over the cube—that is, the multisets

$$\left\{ (T_0(v), \ldots , T_{\mu - 1}(v)) \mid v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell \right\}$$ and $$\left\{ (U_0(v), \ldots , U_{\mu - 1}(v)) \mid v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell \right\}$$,

viewed as multi-subsets of $\mathcal{T}_\tau^\mu$—are equal.

What [[DP23](https://eprint.iacr.org/2023/1784.pdf), § 4] tells us is that to check this claim, _it suffices in turn_ (up to some negligible soundness error) for the verifier to sample a _fingerprinting challenge_ $s$ and a _permutation challenge_ $r$, and to check instead that the virtual polynomials:

$$T^* \coloneqq r + \sum_{i = 0}^{\mu - 1} s^i \cdot T_i$$ and $$U^* \coloneqq r + \sum_{i = 0}^{\mu - 1} s^i \cdot U_i$$

have identical _grand products_. That is, it's enough to check that $\prod_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell} T^*(v) \stackrel{?}= \prod_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell} U^*(v)$.
