# Cryptographic Reductions [Reducing multiset-equality and vanishing claims to evaluation claims]
 
In this section, we describe Binius's cryptographic reductions. These reductions are designed to _simplify_ and _process_ cryptographic claims—about the vanishing of polynomials, or the equality of multisets, for example—and to reduce them to simpler ones. Ultimately, we want to reduce everything to _evaluation claims_, amenable to treatment at the hands of our [polynomial commitment scheme](/cryptography).

Thus, our cryptographic reductions represent a sort of "conveyor belt" or "middleware", that sits between our arithmetization layer and our PCS. They occupy a critical intermediate territory in Binius, and represent a significant source of cryptographic and engineering complexity.

In prime-field SNARKs based on the _DEEP-ALI_ paradigm—like [Plonky3](https://github.com/Plonky3/Plonky3) and [Stwo](https://github.com/starkware-libs/stwo)—the need for reductions like ours [doesn't quite exist](background). Those schemes essentially support _just_ tables, and not channels, and _don't_ support virtual columns. Moreover, they contain native mechanisms that serve to reduce vanishing claims to evaluation claims.

In Binius, we must enrich this model, for various reasons. First, we rely heavily on [virtual polynomials](virtual), polynomials which "sit on top of"—and modify—underlying committed polynomials. Virtual polynomials may be compositions or concatenations, say, of raw polynomials; they may also modify their underlying polynomials in more-complex ways—by _shifting_ or _packing_ them, for example. The _virtual polynomial_ abstraction appears implicitly in _[HyperPlonk_](https://eprint.iacr.org/2022/1355)_, and is developed heavily in Section 4 of _[Succinct Arguments in Towers of Binary Fields](https://eprint.iacr.org/2023/1784)_.

Secondly, we use M3. If we _just_ had tables—like the AIR and PLONKish arithmetizations schemes do—then we would _just_ need a zerocheck protocol, which would play in our setting a role analogous to that played by the _vanishing argument_ in DEEP-ALI (see e.g. [[Hab22](https://eprint.iacr.org/2022/1216.pdf), Prot. 3]). As it stands, however, we have channel-balancing too to deal with, and must devise a way to check the equality of multisets. (These multisets are themselves often heavily virtual, as we've noted already.)

Putting these facts together, we find in Binius a much-richer landscape of _intermediate_ cryptographic reductions than other SNARKs have. We explain these thoroughly throughout the following subsections.

## Section Directory

This section contains the following subsections:
- [**Background.**](background) Here, we explain prior works, and how ours differs from them.
- [**Virtual columns.**](virtual) Binius makes extensive use of _virtual columns_, polynomials which depend algebraically on committed columns. Departing from previous works, we allow even virtual polynomials whose evaluation schemes are _interactive_, in that they reduce to their underlying polynomials' evaluation schemes only with the aid of an interactive protocol (e.g., a sumcheck). This notion proves critical in Binius, as we explain in that page.
- [**Zerocheck.**](zerocheck) The _zerocheck_ protocol is designed to process vanishing claims, like those associated with table constraints in M3. This step intakes a _zerocheck claim_—that is, a claim whereby a certain composition of multilinears vanishes identically over the cube—and reduces that claim to a set of _evaluation claims_, themselves pertaining to the various polynomials (both raw and virtual) underlying the initial composition. In this setting, we use sophisticated techniques tailored to the case of _small-field polynomials_—a research area initiated by Angus Gruen's _[Some Improvements for the PIOP for ZeroCheck](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/108)_.
- [**Multiset check.**](multiset) Here, we discuss how to process _multiset equality_ claims, a key sort of claim associated with the balancing of channels.
- [**GKR-based grand product.**](gpa) _Grand-product claims_ pertain to the _products_ of the respective values taken by polynomials over the cube. These arise as internal reduction targets of our multiset protocol.
- [**Evalcheck.**](evalcheck) Our _evalcheck_ protocol is designed to replace evaluation claims on _virtual_ multilinears by evaluation claims on _concrete_ ones.
