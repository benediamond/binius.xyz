# Evalcheck [Unrolling evaluation claims on virtual multilinears]

Throughout the entirety of the [reductions](/reductions) site section thus far, our task has been to reduce complex mathematical claims to _evaluation claims_. At this point, we have attained this goal: our only remaining claims are evaluation claims on multilinears.

However, one complication remains: the multilinears which these claims pertain to are, in general, [virtual](/reductions/virtual). Almost by definition, virtual polynomials cannot be evaluated _directly_; rather, they must be "unwound": in order to process an evaluation claim on a virtual multilinear, the verifier must perform some further steps. These steps will entail some mix of succinct local computation, interaction with the prover, and so on; the end result will be a _further_ evaluation claim, this time on the polynomial or polynomials that underlie the virtual polynomial.

:::info
As a key representative example, we refer again to the _[shift](/reductions/virtual/shift)_ virtual polynomial. To resolve an evaluation claim of the form $s \stackrel{?}= \texttt{shift}_{b, o}(t)(r)$, the verifier must engage in an $\ell$-round _sumcheck_ with the prover, and then locally evaluate the _shift indicator polynomial_; only at this point, will it output a _further_ claim $s' \stackrel{?}= t(r')$. The point is that we've reduced a claim on something virtual to a claim on something committed.
:::

In certain cases, the ensuring claims will be on further polynomials that are _themselves_ virtual; in this case, the process must be repeated.
