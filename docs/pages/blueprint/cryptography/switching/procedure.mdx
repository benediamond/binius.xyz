# The Procedure [Expressing small-field claims using tensors and sums]

Here, we come to our description of ring-switching. Recall what our goal is: we start with a _huge-field_ polynomial $t(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$ over $\mathcal{T}_\tau$; we assume that we have [committed to](/blueprint/cryptography/commitment) it. Our goal is to evaluate _not_ $t$, but rather its $\kappa$-fold [refinement](/blueprint/cryptography/switching/refinement) $t'(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$, a polynomial over $\mathcal{T}_{\tau - \kappa}$. Here, $\kappa \in \{0, \ldots , \tau\}$ is a parameter.

Thus, we fix an _evaluation point_ $(r_0, \ldots, r_{\ell + \kappa - 1}) \in \mathcal{T}_\tau^{\ell + \kappa}$. Our goal is to learn $t'(r_0, \ldots, r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$. More specifically, our goal is to _reduce_ the problem of learning $t'(r_0, \ldots, r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$ to that of learning $t(r'_0, \ldots , r'_{\ell - 1})$, for some _further_ point $(r'_0, \ldots , r'_{\ell - 1}) \in \mathcal{T}_\tau^\ell$. In words: we want to reduce the problem of evaluating a refinement of $t$ to the problem of evaluating $t$ itself.

## Why This is Hard

Given the ability to evaluate $t(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$, how do we bootstrap that into the ability to evaluate its refinement $t'(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$? This seems like a tricky problem, because the latter task involves dealing "individually" with _substrings_ of $t$'s coefficients, in a way that goes beyond what we're allowed to do when we merely evaluate $t$. Indeed, evaluating $t(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$ [amounts to](/blueprint/primer) dotting $t$'s Lagrange coefficient vector $\left( t(v) \right)_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell}$ with some tensor $\bigotimes_{i = 0}^{\ell - 1} (1 - r'_i, r'_i)$, a "coarse" operation. What we _want_ to do is dot the refinement $\left( t'(v) \right)_{v \in \mathcal{B}_{\ell + \kappa}}$ with a longer tensor $\bigotimes_{i = 0}^{\ell + \kappa - 1} (1 - r_i, r_i)$. How do we gain this "refined" sort of access, given only evaluation access to $t$ itself?

As an exercise, you can think about trying to "lift" $t$-evaluation to $t'$-evaluation. What if the verifier truncated its desired evaluation point $(r_0, \ldots, r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$—and learned $t(r_\kappa, \ldots , r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$, say—would this information, on its own, be enough for the verifier to compute its _desired_ quantity $t'(r_0, \ldots , r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$? As we argue in [[DP24](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/504)], the answer is _no_; it's an instructive exercise to sit on this for a bit.
