# The Procedure [Expressing small-field claims using tensors and sums]

Here, without further ado, we describe the steps of ring-switching. Here, we have an $\ell$-variate polynomial $t(X_0, \ldots, X_{\ell - 1})$ fixed and [committed to](/blueprint/cryptography/commitment); we are going to give a protocol to evaluate its $\kappa$-fold _[refinement](/blueprint/cryptography/switching/refinement)_ $t'(X_0, \ldots, X_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$—specifically, to process an _evaluation claim_ of the form $s \stackrel{?}= t'(r_0, \ldots, r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$.

Our goal will be to reduce that initial evaluation claim to a sum claim on $t(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$—i.e., of the form $s_0 \stackrel{?}= \sum_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell} t(v) \cdot A(v)$, where $A(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$ is some further, transparent, multilinear.

## The Steps

Here is the algorithm.

- By embedding the original (packed) multilinear $t(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$ componentwise _horizontally_ into $A$, we can express it as an $\ell$-variate multilinear over $A$—$\varphi_1(t)$, let's say. The prover computes and sends to the verifier

  $$
  \begin{equation*}\hat{s} \coloneqq \varphi_1(t)(\varphi_0(r_\kappa), \ldots, \varphi_0(r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})).\end{equation*}
  $$
  - The element $\hat{s}$ is an _algebra element_, since the entire evaluation above takes place in the algebra. Thus, concretely, $\hat{s}$ looks like a $2^\kappa \times 2^\kappa$ array of $\mathcal{T}_\iota$-elements.
  - It is a bit tricky, but a very important and doable exercise, to study concretely what $\hat{s}$ looks like. In fact, its _columns_ $\left( \hat{s}_v \right)_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\kappa}$ will be exactly the _partial evaluations_ $\hat{s}_v = t'(v_0, \ldots, v_{\kappa - 1}, r_\kappa, \ldots , r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$. Roughly, this is because the vertically embedded elements $\varphi_0(r_\kappa), \ldots, \varphi_0(r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$ will act "slice-wise" on the cells of the coefficients $\varphi_1(t)(w)$, for $w \in \mathcal{B}_\ell$ (recall that these coefficients are _horizontally_ embedded). We prove this fact rigorously in our paper (see [[DP24](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/504), Thm. 4.2]).
- Writing $\left( \hat{s}_v \right)_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\kappa}$ for $\hat{s}$'s columns, the verifier checks:

  $$
  \begin{equation*}s \stackrel{?}= \sum_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\kappa} \hat{s}_v \cdot \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(r_0, \ldots, r_{\kappa - 1}, v_0, \ldots , v_{\kappa - 1}).\end{equation*}
  $$

  - By the discussion just above, we can immediately see why this check is complete. Indeed, taking on faith for now that $\hat{s}_v = t'(v_0, \ldots, v_{\kappa - 1}, r_\kappa, \ldots , r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$ will hold for each $v \in \mathcal{B}_\kappa$ if the prover is honest, we thus see that the right-hand side above will equal $\sum_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\kappa} t'(v_0, \ldots, v_{\kappa - 1}, r_\kappa, \ldots , r_{\ell + \kappa - 1}) \cdot \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(r_0, \ldots, r_{\kappa - 1}, v_0, \ldots , v_{\kappa - 1}) = t'(r_0, \ldots , r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$; this quantity in turn equals $s$ precisely when the prover's initial evaluation claim is true.
- The verifier samples row-batching scalars $(r''_0, \ldots , r''_{\kappa - 1}) \gets \mathcal{T}_\tau^\kappa$. The verifier ships these off to the prover. Meanwhile, locally, it decomposes $\hat{s}$ into _rows_ $\left( \hat{s}_u \right)_{u \in \mathcal{B}_\kappa}$, and computes the row-combination:

  $$
  \begin{equation*}s_0 \coloneqq \sum_{u \in \mathcal{B}_\kappa} \hat{s}_u \cdot \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(r''_0, \ldots , r''_{\kappa - 1}, u_0, \ldots , u_{\kappa - 1}).\end{equation*}
  $$

  In parallel, the verifier conjures into its head an odd sort of multilinear $A(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$, defined in the following way. For each $w \in \mathcal{B}_\ell$, we have a basis-decomposition:

  $$
  \begin{equation*}\widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(r_\kappa, \ldots , r_{\ell + \kappa - 1}, w_0, \ldots , w_{\ell - 1}) = \sum_{u \in \mathcal{B}_\kappa} A_{w, u} \cdot \beta_u.\end{equation*}
  $$

  I.e., whatever the left-hand $\mathcal{T}_\tau$-element above happens to be, surely it has a coordinate decomposition with respect to the usual $\mathcal{T}_\iota$-basis of $\mathcal{T}_\tau$; that coordinate decomposition is what appears on the right. Finally, the verifier _defines_ $A(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$ to be the multilinear extension of the function $A : \mathcal{B}_\ell \to \mathcal{T}_\tau$ that sends:

  $$
  \begin{equation*}A : w \mapsto \sum_{u \in \mathcal{B}_\kappa} A_{w, u} \cdot \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(r''_0, \ldots , r''_{\kappa - 1}, u_0, \ldots , u_{\kappa - 1}).\end{equation*}
  $$
    - This might sound crazy, but the verifier isn't _doing_ any of these compositions. Indeed, that wouldn't be succinct at all! Rather, the verifier is simply _defining_ this polynomial in its head right now, not evaluating it anywhere yet (let alone on the entire cube).
    - Indeed, as it turns out, the verifier _will_ ultimately evaluate $A(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$ at exactly one point—that point will show up during the processing of the sum claim we're about to output. Details are [here](/blueprint/cryptography/evaluation).
- We're done! The verifier now outputs the _sum claim_
  $$
  \begin{equation*}s_0 \stackrel{?}= \sum_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell} t(v) \cdot A(v).\end{equation*}
  $$
    - Above, we agreed that we wanted to output a sum claim of the above form for which $A(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$ is _transparent_. Indeed, it's a far-from-obvious fact—which we prove in our paper—[[DP24](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/504)]—that $A(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$ _is transparent_. Indeed, the verifier can evaluate it at any point $(r'_0, \ldots , r'_{\ell - 1})$ by just doing $O(\ell)$ $\mathcal{T}_\tau$-multiplications.
    - This latter fact is where the tensor algebra really shines. Indeed, we don't know how to succinctly evaluate $A(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$ _without_ exploiting the algebraic structure of the tensor algebra!
