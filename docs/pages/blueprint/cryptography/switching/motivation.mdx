# Motivational Remarks [Setting the stage for ring-switching]

Recall what our goal is: we start with a _huge-field_ polynomial $t(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$ over $\mathcal{T}_\tau$; we assume that we have [committed to](/blueprint/cryptography/commitment) it. Our goal is to evaluate _not_ $t$, but rather its $\kappa$-fold [refinement](/blueprint/cryptography/switching/refinement) $t'(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$, a polynomial over $\mathcal{T}_{\tau - \kappa}$. Here, $\kappa \in \{0, \ldots , \tau\}$ is a parameter.

Thus, we fix an _evaluation point_ $(r_0, \ldots, r_{\ell + \kappa - 1}) \in \mathcal{T}_\tau^{\ell + \kappa}$. Our goal is to learn $t'(r_0, \ldots, r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$. More specifically, our goal is to _reduce_ the problem of learning $t'(r_0, \ldots, r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$ to that of learning $t(r'_0, \ldots , r'_{\ell - 1})$, for some _further_ point $(r'_0, \ldots , r'_{\ell - 1}) \in \mathcal{T}_\tau^\ell$. In words: we want to reduce the problem of evaluating a refinement of $t$ to the problem of evaluating $t$ itself.

## Why This is Hard

Given the ability to evaluate $t(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$, how do we bootstrap that into the ability to evaluate its refinement $t'(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$? This seems like a tricky problem, because the latter task involves dealing "individually" with _substrings_ of $t$'s coefficients, in a way that goes beyond what we're allowed to do when we merely evaluate $t$ in a blackbox way. Indeed, evaluating $t(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$ [amounts to](/blueprint/primer) dotting $t$'s Lagrange coefficient vector $\left( t(v) \right)_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell}$ with some tensor $\bigotimes_{i = 0}^{\ell - 1} (1 - r'_i, r'_i)$, a "coarse" operation. What we _want_ to do is dot the refined vector $\left( t'(v) \right)_{v \in \mathcal{B}_{\ell + \kappa}}$ with a longer tensor $\bigotimes_{i = 0}^{\ell + \kappa - 1} (1 - r_i, r_i)$. How do we gain this "refined" sort of access, given only evaluation access to $t$ itself?

As an exercise, you can think about trying to "lift" $t$-evaluation to $t'$-evaluation. The simplest possibility would be if the verifier could, by evaluating $t$ _at just a single point_—and doing some amount of local work, let's say—thereby evaluate $t'$. To learn its desired thing $t'(r_0, \ldots, r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$, where should the verifier evaluate $t$? Maybe at the truncated point $(r_\kappa, \ldots , r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$—would this information, on its own, be enough? As we argue in [[DP24](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/504)], the answer is _no_; and nor can _any single evaluation_ of $t$ be enough _on its own_ (i.e., without interaction with the prover, and so on).

In [this note](https://hackmd.io/@benediamond/BkLYpf__0), we show how $2^\kappa$ evaluations of $t$ at _different points_—specifically, at a componentwise Galois orbit of $(r_\kappa, \ldots , r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$—are enough to learn $t'(r_0, \ldots, r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$. The approach we pursue—namely, ring-switching—gives an alternative to the Galois-theoretic one, which turns out to be more efficient than that one is.

Indeed, while evaluating $t$ at $(r_\kappa, \ldots , r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$ alone is indeed _not_ enough, it _becomes_ enough if we operate over a _larger ring_ that contains $\mathcal{T}_\tau$ as a subring. In fact, that larger ring is nothing other than the [tensor algebra](/blueprint/cryptography/switching/tensor).

## Sum Claims

Actually, for technical reasons, we are going to process our evaluation claim $s \stackrel{?}= t'(r_0, \ldots, r_{\ell + \kappa - 1})$ in two steps. First, we will reduce our initial evaluation claim to a _sum claim_ about the underlying polynomial $t(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$. By definition, a _sum claim_ amounts to a claim of the form

$$
\begin{equation*}s_0 \stackrel{?}= \sum_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\ell} t(v) \cdot A(v),\end{equation*}
$$

where $A(X_0, \ldots, X_{\ell - 1})$ is _some other_ $\ell$-variate multilinear over $\mathcal{T}_\tau$ (in particular, it's a _[transparent](/blueprint/reductions/virtual#terminal-examples)_ polynomial that the verifier can simply evaluate).

This kind of reduction amounts to progress, since we can always use the _sumcheck protocol_ on claims of that latter sort.

:::note
In fact, in practice, we will _combine_ this sumcheck into our evaluation procedure, as we explain thoroughly [here](/blueprint/cryptography/evaluation); this technicality is irrelevant for now.
:::

Thus, for now, it's enough to reduce our evaluation claim on $t'$ to a _sum claim_ on $t$. This is actually what we will [set out to do](/blueprint/cryptography/switching/procedure) in ring-switching.
