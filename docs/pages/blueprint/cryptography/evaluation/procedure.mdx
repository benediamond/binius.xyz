# The Procedure [Our protocol for batch evaluation of sum claims]

Here, we get to the "grand finale" of the site. Our setup is a list of polynomials $t_0, \ldots , t_{n - 1}$, respectively of sizes $\ell_0, \ldots , \ell_{n - 1}$, all defined over $\mathcal{T}_\tau$, and _jointly committed_ to in the way prescribed by our [batch commitment](/blueprint/cryptography/commitment/batched) procedure. That is, we've _actually_ committed to just a single, massive, piecewise polynomial $t^*(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell^* - 1})$, which takes the values that the individual polynomials $t_0, \ldots , t_{n - 1}$ respectively do on various _subcubes_ of $\mathcal{B}_{\ell^*}$ (and zero-fills the remaining vertices).

We moreover fix a list of _sum claims_, each of the form
$$
\begin{equation*}s_{i, 0} \stackrel{?}= \sum_{v \in \mathcal{B}_{\ell_i}} t_i(v) \cdot A_i(v).\end{equation*}
$$

Here, the $A_i(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell_i - 1})$ are further _transparent_ polynomials that arise during the ring-switching reduction. We don't need to worry what these polynomials _even are_ for the purposes of this page; we merely need the fact that they're transparent (i.e., efficiently evaluable by the verifier at any given point). Our goal in this page will be to jointly evaluate all of these sum claims.

## The Steps

We now record our evaluation protocol. We drop a slightly simplified variant in which a certain _oracle skipping_ optimization is omitted. (We refer to [[DP24](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/504), § 6.2] for a description of this optimization.)

We write $f^{(0)} : S^{(0)} \to \mathcal{T}_\tau$ for the result of the prover's _initial_ [commitment](/blueprint/cryptography/commitment/procedure). Here, $S^{(0)} \subset \mathcal{T}_\tau$ is a _Reed–Solomon domain_ of size $2^{\ell^* + \mathcal{R}}$. We moreover fix the [usual](/blueprint/cryptography/evaluation/fri) sequence of further FRI domains $S^{(0)}, \ldots , S^{(\ell^*)}$, as well as the 2-to-1 folding maps $q^{(i)} : S^{(i)} \to S^{(i + 1)}$. Using these, we get a FRI folding procedure, which, given a word $f^{(i)} : S^{(i)} \to mathcal{T}_\tau$ and a folding challenge $r_i \in \mathcal{T}_\tau$, produces a folded word $f^{(i + 1)} \coloneqq \mathsf{fold}(f^{(i)}, r_i)$, a map $f^{(i + 1)} : S^{(i + 1)} \to \mathcal{T}_\tau$.
