# Batch Evaluation [Batch-evaluating big-field sum claims of unequal sizes]

We've officially arrived at the very last step in the protocol. As of this point, thanks to our [ring-switching](/blueprint/cryptography/switching) reduction, we have brought the outstanding content we _still need to prove_ down to a list of _sum claims_ pertaining to various polynomials—all defined over the single, huge field $\mathcal{T}_\tau$, though generally of _different_ numbers of variables.

In this subsection, we describe how to _batch-process_ sum claims of this form. Our technique is an extension of that described in the academic work [[DP24](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/504.pdf)]. Indeed, that work only describes the commitment of a single polynomial. To extend that work to the batched setting—and in particular, to handle many polynomials, of _unequal sizes_—we need to exert some extra effort.

The essential efficiency property will be that we will only need to run FRI _once_, on a single committed polynomial (namely, that described in our [batch commitment](/blueprint/cryptography/commitment/batched) procedure).

## Subsection Directory

This subsection contains the following pages:

- [**Review of FRI.**](fri) Here, we drop a crash course on the _FRI_ protocol [[BBHR18](https://eccc.weizmann.ac.il/report/2017/134/)], a crucial proximity test for Reed–Solomon codes. We also describe a _particular instantiation_ of that protocol—due to [[DP24](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/504.pdf)]—designed to play nicely with the [additive NTT](/blueprint/cryptography/commitment/additive).
- [**Front-Loaded Sumcheck.**](frontloaded) While batching sumchecks on _equally-sized_ polynomials is standard, things get trickier when the polynomials have unequal numbers of variables. Here, we describe our protocol for this sort of thing, which we call the _front-loaded sumcheck_.
- [**Evaluating Piecewise Multilinears.**](piecewise) Here, we continue our treatment of unequally-sized polynomials. While piecewise multilinears _all of whose pieces are the same size_ are [straightforward](/blueprint/reductions/virtual/merge) to deal with, things get much more interesting when the constituent "pieces" are unequally sized.
- [**The Procedure.**](procedure) Here, we describe our main evaluation procedure, which combines a "front-loaded sumcheck" with FRI folding.
