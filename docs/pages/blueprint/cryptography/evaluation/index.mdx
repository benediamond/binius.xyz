# Batch Evaluation [Batch-evaluating big-field sum claims of unequal sizes]

We've officially arrived at the very last step in the protocol. As of this point, thanks to our [ring-switching](/blueprint/cryptography/switching) reduction, we have brought our outstanding claims down to a list of _sum claims_ pertaining to various polynomials over the huge field $\mathcal{T}_\tau$. That is, for some list length $n$, we have a sequence of claims of the form

$$
\begin{equation*}s_{i, 0} \stackrel{?}= \sum_{v \in \mathcal{B}_{\ell_i}} t_i(v) \cdot A_i(v).\end{equation*}
$$

Here, the individual polynomials $t_i(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell_i - 1})$ are all defined over the huge field $\mathcal{T}_\tau$, though their numbers of variables $\ell_i$ are not necessarily the same; the $A_i(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell_i - 1})$ are further _transparent_ polynomials that arise during the ring-switching reduction.

In this subsection, we describe how to batch-process sum claims of this form. Our technique is an _extension_ of that described in the academic work [[DP24](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/504.pdf)]. Indeed, that work only describes the commitment of a single polynomial. To extend that work to the batched setting—and in particular, to handle polynomials of _unequal sizes_—we need to do exert some extra effort.
