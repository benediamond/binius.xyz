# The Backend [The full explanation of our cryptography]

This site area contains the full mathematical specification of Binius64. Our section on the [shift indicators](/blueprint/backend/shifts) contains key mathematical ideas that will get used throughout. Our sections on the [AND](/blueprint/backend/ands) and [MUL](/blueprint/backend/muls) reductions essentially operate independently of each other, though both depend on the shift indicators. Both of those reductions target the [shift reduction](/blueprint/backend/reduction), which can be thought of as a common internal subroutine, the "workhorse" of Binius64. That reduction _in turn_ outputs an evaluation claim, directly amenable to our [PCS](/blueprint/commitment/).

Throughout the entirety of this site area, we assume that the prover has begun by committing his [prover data](/blueprint/constraints/indices#prover-data) using our [PCS](/blueprint/commitment/).

Here, we break down in more detail this area's sections.

- [**The Shift Indicators.**](shifts) Binius64 natively enshrines _[shifted value indices](/blueprint/constraints/indices)_ into its constraint system. In order to assess the validity of prover claims that involve shifted data, we need to make shifts mathematical. In this site section, we answer a key question: how does the multilinear extension of shifted data relate to the multilinear extension of the original data? Our treatment of this problem builds on Diamond and Posen [[DP25](https://eprint.iacr.org/2023/1784), § 4.3].
- [**The AND Reduction.**](ands) To assess whether the constraint system's [AND constraints](/blueprint/constraints/ands) hold over the prover's shifted witness data, we recast that question as one about the vanishing of a multivariate polynomial over $\mathbb{F}_2$. To assess that vanishing problem, we in turn use an advanced adaptation of Gruen's [[Gru24](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/108), § 5.1] _[univariate skip](/blueprint/backend/ands/univariate)_ protocol, which also uses ideas of Dao and Thaler [[DT24a](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/1038), [DT24b](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/1210)]. Our implementation of our prover works "when possible" in the 8-bit [Rijndael field](/blueprint/backend/ands/rijndael); we call it the _[Rijndael zerocheck](/blueprint/backend/ands/implementation)_.
- [**The MUL Reduction.**](mul) To assess whether our prover's [MUL constraints](/blueprint/constraints/muls) hold, we need a way of obtaining unsigned 64-bit integer multiplication within the context of binary fields. To complete.
- [**The Shift Reduction.**](reduction) The shift reduction is the technical core of Binius64. That reduction picks up where _both_ the AND and MUL reductions leave off: namely, with evaluation claims on certain [oblong-multilinearizations](/blueprint/math/oblong) of polynomials that depend on the prover's data. To assess those evaluation claims, we undertake a two-stage sumcheck, which roughly evokes Spartan [[Set20](https://eprint.iacr.org/2019/550)]'s. In the process, we need to use [shifted witness polynomials](/blueprint/backend/shifts/polynomials) throughout.
