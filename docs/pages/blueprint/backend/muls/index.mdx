# The MUL Reduction [Using GKR to handle unsigned integer multiplication]

Here, we turn to our prover's [MUL constraints](/blueprint/constraints/muls). Our multiplication protocol is the second, large "entrypoint protocol" in Binius64. Like our [AND reduction](/blueprint/backend/ands), our MUL reduction ingests the constraint system, and reduces the prover's satisfaction of these constraints—its MUL constraints, this time—to evaluation claims on [oblong-multilinearizations](/blueprint/math/oblong) of the prover's [constraint arrays](/blueprint/constraints/muls#the-constraint-arrays). Claims of this latter form are exactly what our [shift reduction](/blueprint/backend/reduction) is designed to treat, so we defer the problem of the assessment of _those_ evaluation claims' validity to that reduction. We note that the [shift reduction](/blueprint/backend/reduction) is a common reduction target of both the AND and MUL reductions.

Our MUL reduction is mathematically involved, and builds on two key ideas. First, we work "in the exponent"; second, we use GKR. The content of this section can be viewed as an improved variant of an idea previously put out in an [Irreducible blog post](https://www.irreducible.com/posts/integer-multiplication-in-binius).
- [**Multiplying in the Exponent.**](multiplying) Since $(g^r)^s = g^{r \cdot s}$ holds for each ring-element $g$ and each pair of integers $r$ and $s$, we can hope to reduce the condition $r \cdot s \stackrel{?}= t$ instead to the condition $(g^r)^s \stackrel{?}= g^t$. Here, we work out the details of this reduction.
- [**Exponentiating Multilinears.**](exponentiating) This is the GKR-based core of our multiplication reduction. Here, we show how to use GKR to evaluate the multilinear extension of a multilinear defined pointwise, on the cube, as the exponentiation of a variable base by a variable exponent.
- [**Combined Protocol.**](combined) Here, we give an outline of our full multiplication protocol. The idea is mainly to combine the previous to parts, but there are details to work out.
