# Merging [Building large polynomials out of smaller ones]

As an extremely basic example, let's say we have 2 $\ell$-variate things $t_0(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$ and $t_1(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1})$. Equivalently, we have two lists $\left( t_{0, 0}, \ldots , t_{0, 2^\ell - 1} \right)$ and $\left( t_{1, 0}, \ldots , t_{1, 2^\ell - 1} \right)$. What if we want the $\ell + 1$-variate polynomial—say, $t^*(X_0, \ldots , X_\ell)$—associated with the concatenation $\left( t_{0, 0}, \ldots , t_{0, 2^\ell - 1}, t_{1, 0}, \ldots , t_{1, 2^\ell - 1} \right)$? To cut to the chase, we can write down the algebraic expression:

$$
\begin{equation*}t^*(X_0, \ldots , X_\ell) = (1 - X_\ell) \cdot t_0(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1}) + X_\ell \cdot t_1(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1}).\end{equation*}
$$

It is a basic exercise that the list representation of _this_ polynomial $t^*(X_0, \ldots , X_\ell)$ indeed gives us the concatenation of the two original lists that we started with.

The final thing to note is that the verifier may evaluate $t^*(r)$ efficiently, for any point $(r_0, \ldots , r_\ell) \in \mathcal{T}_\tau^{\ell + 1}$. How? To do this, the verifier should first query $s_0 \coloneqq t_0(r_0, \ldots , r_{\ell - 1})$ and $s_1 \coloneqq t_1(r_0, \ldots , r_{\ell - 1})$. Finally, the verifier should locally compute the combination $(1 - r_\ell) \cdot s_0 + r_\ell \cdot s_1$ and output it. Indeed, this evidently gives us an evaluation procedure for $t^*$ (entailing two sub-queries to $t_0$ and $t_1$ and a tiny bit of local math).

Finally, there is a geometrical story corresponding to this concatenation operation. As the equation above makes clear, $t^*$, as a polynomial, is defined "piecewise" on the $\ell + 1$-dimensional cube. That is, we let the "bottom" $\ell$-subcube of that cube (i.e., where $X_\ell = 0$) take exactly the values that $t_0$ does, and the "top" $\ell$-subcube (i.e., where $X_\ell = 1$) take exactly the values that $t_1$ does. The resulting piecewise polynomial takes, as its values, the concatenation of the values that $t_0$ and $t_1$ respectively take.

## Concatenating

It is not hard to generalize this basic procedure to some power-of-two number $2^\alpha$ of constituent polynomials, instead of just 2. We'll fix $\ell$ again, and consider now $\ell$-variate constituent polynomials $t_0, \ldots , t_{2^\alpha - 1}$. To concatenate _all_ of these things into a single, $2^{\ell + \alpha}$-sized list, we want the composite polynomial:

$$t^*(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell + \alpha - 1}) \coloneqq \sum_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\alpha} t_{\{v\}}(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell - 1}) \cdot \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(X_\ell, \ldots , X_{\ell + \alpha - 1}, v_0, \ldots , v_{\alpha - 1})$$.

For straightforward reasons, the table of values of $t^*$ over the $\ell + \alpha$-dimensional cube yields exactly the concatenation of the polynomials $t_0, \ldots , t_{2^\alpha - 1}$'s respective individual tables of values. Geometrically, here were are partitioning $\mathcal{B}_{\ell + \alpha}$ into $2^\alpha$ $\ell$-dimensional subcubes, and defining $t^*$ piecewise on $\mathcal{B}_{\ell + \alpha}$ out of the $t_0, \ldots , t_{2^\alpha - 1}$.

The important thing here is that if $2^\alpha$ is small, then this thing is succinct for the verifier. Indeed, to evaluate $t^*$ at some arbitrary point $(r_0, \ldots , r_{\ell + \alpha - 1}) \in \mathcal{T}_\tau^{\ell + \alpha}$, the verifier may first query $s_v \coloneqq t_{\{v\}}(r_0, \ldots , r_{\ell - 1})$, for each $v \in \mathcal{B}_\alpha$. Next, the verifier must simply output the combination $\sum_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\alpha} s_v \cdot \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(v_0, \ldots , v_{\alpha - 1}, r_\ell, \ldots , r_{\ell + \alpha - 1})$. By a standard algorithm, the verifier may compute the vector $\left( \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(v_0, \ldots , v_{\alpha - 1}, r_\ell, \ldots , r_{\ell + \alpha - 1}) \right)_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\alpha}$—and hence the entire sum above—in $\Theta(2^\alpha)$ time.

## Interleaving

By changing this story very slightly, we can also achieve a dual outcome, whereby we _interleave_ $2^\alpha$ $\ell$-variate polynomials. That is, we construct an $\ell + \alpha$-variate polynomial whose (lexicographically flattened) list of evaluations is exactly the interleaving of the respective lists of $2^\alpha$ constituent polynomials $t_0, \ldots , t_{2^\alpha - 1}$.

To do this, we just need to switch the endianness of our combination operation—that is, we need to combine these polynomials using the _low_ variables as "selectors", instead of the high variables. In other words, we need to use the alternate expression:

$$
\begin{equation*}t^*(X_0, \ldots , X_{\ell + \alpha - 1}) \coloneqq \sum_{v \in \mathcal{B}_\alpha} t_{\{v\}}(X_\alpha, \ldots , X_{\ell + \alpha - 1}) \cdot \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(X_0, \ldots , X_{\alpha - 1}, v_0, \ldots , v_{\alpha - 1}).\end{equation*}
$$

This thing is also succinct for the verifier to evaluate (provided $2^\alpha$ is small), for the same reason that the concatenated polynomial was.
