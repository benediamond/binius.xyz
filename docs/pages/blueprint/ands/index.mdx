# AND Constraints [Evaluating bitwise ANDs of 64-bit words]

Here, we turn to the problem of cryptographically checking whether some fixed list of [AND constraints](/blueprint/constraints/ands) actually holds over prover data $w$. To do this, we need to do a number of cryptographic steps. First, we need to arithmetize the problem using multilinears. Next, we need to oblong-multilinearize everything, and run the univariate skip.

These steps serve to reduce our initial constraint-satisfaction problem to a lower-level one, involving evaluation claims on certain multilinears _related_ to the witness. Next, we need to further reduce those claims, until they become claims about the witness itself.

We explain these steps in detail throughout the below pages.

- [**The Shift Indicators.**](indicators) In this page, we prepare a key mathematical building block. These are polynomials whose restrictions to the cube "indicate" arithmetic offsets. In this effort, we wind up generalizing and building off of [[DP25](https://eprint.iacr.org/2023/1784), § 4.3].
- [**Mathematizing the Problem.**](mathematizing) Equipped with our shift indicators, we explain how to recast the question of a witness's satisfaction of a constraint system's [AND constraints](/blueprint/constraints/ands) into a problem about multilinear polynomials.
- [**The Univariate Skip.**](univariate) The _univariate skip_ is an efficient protocol for zerochecking polynomials over tiny fields, originally due to Gruen [[Gru24](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/108), § 5.1]. We use a variant of this idea, adapted to our 64-bit setting, which also incorporates ideas of Dao and Thaler [[DT24](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/1038)].
- [**The Shift Reduction.**](reduction) This step takes the _output_ of the univariate skip reduction and further reduces it, until it becomes a claim about the witness. It's a complicated, multi-phase sumcheck. We describe that sumcheck and some considerations around its implementation.
