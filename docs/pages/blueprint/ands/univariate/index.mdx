# The Univariate Skip [Zerocheck variant optimized for 64-bit words]

In the [previous page](/blueprint/ands/mathematizing#multilinearizing), we wrote down a vanishing identity involving $6 + \ell_\text{and}$-variate $\mathbb{F}_2$-polynomials, whose satisfaction captures the prover's satisfaction of the constraint system's AND constraints. In this site section, we reduce that vanishing identity to an _evaluation_ claim on those polynomials.

The standard way to do this is the zerocheck (see e.g. [[CBBZ23](https://eprint.iacr.org/2022/1355.pdf), § 3.2]). On the other hand, our situation is special, since all polynomials involved in that identity have $\mathbb{F}_2$-coefficients. Thus apply a few few more subtle tools at this problem, combining ideas of Gruen [[Gru24](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/108), § 5.1] and Dao and Thaler [[DT24](https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/1038)].

We break our treatment into a few steps, outlined below.
- [**Oblong-Multilinearization.**](multilinearization) Here, we describe the _generic_ mathematical transformation that turns a standard, "cubular" multilinear into an oblong one, i.e. which takes prescribed values on a _rectangular_ domain.
- [**The Rijndael Field.**](rijndael) Here, we explain the _Rijndael field_, a small, 8-bit field used in AES (for example). We also prepare mathematical preliminaries, explaining that field's interplay with the 128-bit GHASH field.
- [**The Protocol.**](protocol) Here, we record the overall structure of our univariate skip variant, and prove its soundness. We adopt the verifier's viewpoint, and purposefully refrain from explaining the prover's implementation.
- [**Prover Implementation.**](implementation) Here, we explain our prover's implementation. Our implementation is highly efficient, and uses the Rijndael field "where possible".
