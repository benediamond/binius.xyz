# Implementation Considerations [Implementing the univariate skip efficiently on 64-bit architectures]

We recall that the prover's goal is to evaluate the polynomial

$$
\begin{equation*}g(\widehat{I}) = \sum_{x \in \mathcal{B}_{\ell_\text{and}}} f(\widehat{I}, x) \cdot \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(r_x, x) = \sum_{x \in \mathcal{B}_{\ell_\text{and}}} \left[ \widehat{a}(\widehat{I}, x) \cdot \widehat{b}(\widehat{I}, x) - \widehat{c}(\widehat{I}, x) \right] \cdot \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(r_x, x)\end{equation*}
$$

on at least $2^k - 1$ points outside of $D$.

In this page, we describe a highly optimized implementation, which strategically uses the [Rijndael field](/blueprint/ands/univariate/deterministic#the-rijndael-field) "where possible".

## The Underlying Arrays

The polynomials $\widehat{a}(\widehat{I}, x)$, $\widehat{b}(\widehat{I}, x)$ and $\widehat{c}(\widehat{I}, x)$ above are [defined](/blueprint/ands/univariate/protocol#oblong-multilinearization) to be the [oblong-multilinearizations](/blueprint/ands/univariate/multilinearization) of [explicit polynomials](/blueprint/ands/mathematizing#multilinearizing) that depend on the prover's data. Unwinding what everything means, we see that $\widehat{a}(\widehat{I}, x)$'s, $\widehat{b}(\widehat{I}, x)$'s and $\widehat{c}(\widehat{I}, x)$'s respective tables of values on $D \times \mathcal{B}_{\ell_\text{and}}$ are nothing more than the length-$n_\text{and}$, 64-bit-word-valued [arrays](/blueprint/ands/mathematizing#sum-expressions) $a$, $b$ and $c$ we've already seen. The prover can prepare these tables of values [efficiently](/blueprint/ands/mathematizing#sum-expressions).

## Efficient Lookup-Based Extrapolation

We also saw already that the prover must evaluate $g(\widehat{I})$ on $2^k - 1$ points outside of $D$. Thus far, we have let $D \subset \mathbb{F}_{2^{128}}$ be an abstract, $6$-dimensional subspace. Now, we want to enforce that $D \subset \mathbb{F}_{2^8} \subset \mathbb{F}_{2^{128}}$. We write $D' \subset \mathbb{F}_{2^{128}}$ for a further linear subspace, now of dimension 7. We require that

$$
\begin{equation*}D \subset D' \subset \mathbb{F}_{2^8} \subset \mathbb{F}_{2^{128}}.\end{equation*}
$$
This condition is not hard to set up (we can construct both subspaces in $K$, and embed them).

Finally, we prepare one more step. Below, we will need the mapping

$$
\begin{equation*}\text{Extrap} : \mathbb{F}_2^{64} \to \mathbb{F}_{2^8}^{64}\end{equation*}
$$

that extrapolates a bitstring from $D$ to $D' \setminus D$. On the other hand, we want to work in the Rijndael field $K$, so things get a bit more subtle.

On input a 64-bit word $(\alpha_0, \ldots , \alpha_{63})$, $\text{Extrap}$ interprets that word as the values that some univariate polynomial $p(\widehat{I})$, of degree less than 64, takes on $D \subset \mathbb{F}_{2^{128}}$. (I.e., this $p(\widehat{I})$ happens to take $\mathbb{F}_2$-valued values on $D$.) This data specifies $p(\widehat{I})$ uniquely, as a polynomial in $\mathbb{F}_{2^{128}}[\widehat{I}]^{\prec 64}$. Finally, $\text{Extrap}$ maps:

$$
\begin{equation*}\text{Extrap} : (\alpha_0, \ldots , \alpha_{63}) \mapsto \Big( \iota^{-1}(p(\widehat{i})) \Big)_{\widehat{i} \in D' \setminus D}.\end{equation*}
$$

We note that since $D' \subset \mathbb{F}_{2^{8}}$ and $p(\widehat{I})$ is bit-valued, $p(\widehat{I})$ takes byte-values on $D'$, so the evaluations $p(\widehat{i})$ live in the image of $\iota$ and $\iota^{-1}(p(\widehat{i}))$ is well-defined.

## Round Polynomial Computation

So here is what the prover does, on input $r_{\widehat{i}}$, $r_x$:

- tensor-expand $r_x$; i.e., compute the array $\left( \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(r_x, x) \right)_{x \in \mathcal{B}_{\ell_\text{and}}}$.
- initialize an empty array of $64$ $128$-bit field elements, say $\mathsf{h}'$, indexed by the set $D' \setminus D$.
- **for** each $x \in \{0, \ldots , n_\text{words} - 1\}$ **do**:
  - interpret the three 64-bit words $a[x]$, $b[x]$ and $c[x]$ as the respective vectors of values that three different $\mathbb{F}_2$-valued univariate polynomials, say $a_x(\widehat{I})$, $b_x(\widehat{I})$, and $c_x(\widehat{I})$, each of degree less than $2^k$, take on $D \subset \mathbb{F}_{2^{128}}$. using the Lin–Chung–Han additive NTT [[LCH14](https://arxiv.org/abs/1404.3458)] or some lookup-based variant of it (see e.g. [[Hu+25](https://eprint.iacr.org/2025/717)]), extrapolate $a_x(\widehat{I})$, $b_x(\widehat{I})$, and $c_x(\widehat{I})$ to $D'$. concretely, this gives us three length-64 arrays, each of whose elements is a $\mathbb{F}_{2^{128}}$-element.
  - **for** each $\widehat{i} \in D' \setminus D$ **do**:
    - $\mathsf{h}'[\widehat{i}] \mathrel{+}= \left[ a_x(\widehat{i}) \cdot b_x(\widehat{i}) - c_x(\widehat{i}) \right] \cdot \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(r_x, x)$.
 - interpret $\mathsf{h}'$ as the vector of values that a polynomial $h'(\widehat{I})$, of degree less than $2 \cdot 2^k$, which also vanishes identically on $D$, takes on $D' \setminus D$. using a further variant of the additive NTT [[LCH14](https://arxiv.org/abs/1404.3458)] (this time dealing with a domain of size $3 \cdot 2^k$), extrapolate $h'(\widehat{I})$ onto $D''$.
 - for each $\widehat{i} \in D'' \setminus D$, set $h(\widehat{i}) \coloneqq \delta_D(r_{\widehat{i}}, \widehat{i}) \cdot h'(\widehat{i})$.
 - return the values of $h(\widehat{I})$ on $D'' \setminus D$.

This completes the description of the prover's calculation of the first round polynomial. In practice, we use a lookup technique whereby we break each of the words $a[x]$, $b[x]$ and $c[x]$ into eight 8-bit chunks. Since the additive NTT extrapolation operation we're carrying out is linear, it suffices to precompute eight lookup tables, each containing 256 length-64 arrays of $\mathbb{F}_{2^{128}}$-elements. The total data size here is 2 MiB.

## The Univariate Specialization

As we already saw, at the [end of the initial round](/blueprint/ands/reduction/sumchecks#the-first-phase) of the univariate skip, the verifier will sample a single scalar $r'_{\widehat{i}} \gets \mathbb{F}_{2^{128}}$ and send it to the prover. The parties' "cubular" sumcheck will be run on

$$
\begin{align*}
s_0 &\stackrel{?}= \delta_D(r_{\widehat{i}}, r'_{\widehat{i}}) \cdot \sum_{x \in \mathcal{B}_{\ell_\text{and}}} f(r'_{\widehat{i}}, x) \cdot \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(r_x, x) \\
&= \delta_D(r_{\widehat{i}}, r'_{\widehat{i}}) \cdot \sum_{x \in \mathcal{B}_{\ell_\text{and}}} \left[ \widehat{a}(r'_{\widehat{i}}, x) \cdot \widehat{b}(r'_{\widehat{i}}, x) - \widehat{c}(r'_{\widehat{i}}, x) \right] \cdot \widetilde{\texttt{eq}}(r_x, x).
\end{align*}
$$

To prepare this sumcheck, the prover needs to prepare the tables of values of the partially specialized, $\ell_{\text{and}}$-variate multilinears $\widehat{a}(r'_{\widehat{i}}, X)$, $\widehat{b}(r'_{\widehat{i}}, X)$ and $\widehat{c}(r'_{\widehat{i}}, X)$ on the cube $\mathcal{B}_{\ell_\text{and}}$. We [recall](/blueprint/ands/univariate/protocol) that the polynomials $\widehat{a}(\widehat{I}, X)$, $\widehat{b}(\widehat{I}, X)$ and $\widehat{c}(\widehat{I}, X)$—i.e., pre-specialization—are _themselves_ [oblong-multilinearizations](/blueprint/ands/univariate/multilinearization) of [explicit](/blueprint/ands/mathematizing#using-shift-indicators) $6 + \ell_\text{and}$-variate multilinears $\widetilde{a}(I, X)$, $\widetilde{b}(I, X)$ and $\widetilde{c}(I, X)$.

This brings us to our next question: how do the tables of values of the $\ell_{\text{and}}$-variate multilinears $\widehat{a}(r'_{\widehat{i}}, X)$, $\widehat{b}(r'_{\widehat{i}}, X)$ and $\widehat{c}(r'_{\widehat{i}}, X)$ on $\mathcal{B}_{\ell_\text{and}}$ relate to the tables of values of $\widetilde{a}(I, X)$, $\widetilde{b}(I, X)$ and $\widetilde{c}(I, X)$, which we already know? These latter are just the [simple arrays](/blueprint/ands/mathematizing#sum-expressions) $a$, $b$ and $c$.

We have already [answered this question](/blueprint/ands/univariate/multilinearization#partial-specialization) in the general context of oblong-multilinearization. In our context, we can say a bit more. Now, we have $k = 6$. Moreover, moreover, for each $x \in \mathcal{B}_{\ell_\text{and}}$, the three bit-arrays

$$
\begin{gather*}
\left( \widetilde{a}(i_0, \ldots , i_5, x_0, \ldots , x_{\ell_\text{and} - 1}) \right)_{i \in \mathcal{B}_6}, \\
\left( \widetilde{b}(i_0, \ldots , i_5, x_0, \ldots , x_{\ell_\text{and} - 1}) \right)_{i \in \mathcal{B}_6}, \\
\left( \widetilde{c}(i_0, \ldots , i_5, x_0, \ldots , x_{\ell_\text{and} - 1}) \right)_{i \in \mathcal{B}_6}
\end{gather*}
$$

are stored conveniently as the three 64-bit registers $a[x]$, $b[x]$ and $c[x]$. To run the [specialization algorithm](/blueprint/ands/univariate/multilinearization#partial-specialization) on $a$, $b$ and $c$, the prover just needs to do, for each $x \in \{0, \ldots , n_\text{words} - 1\}$ (in parallel), three size-64 subset sums of $\left( \delta_D(r'_{\widehat{i}}, \widehat{i}) \right)_{i \in \mathcal{B}_6}$, where the respective bit-coefficients are $a[x]$, $b[x]$ and $c[x]$.
